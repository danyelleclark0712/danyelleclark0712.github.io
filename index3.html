City Overview

Springfield, incorporated in 1840, is located in Central Illinois. It is an urban area with a population of approximately 114,000 people, with an average income level on par with the state (U.S. Census Bureau, n.d.).

Top Attractions in Springfield

Top Attractions

Rank Attraction Description
1 Lincoln Home National Historic Site The childhood home of Abraham Lincoln, offering a glimpse into his early life.
2 Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum TA world-class museum dedicated to the life and legacy of Abraham Lincoln.
3 Old State Capitol A historic landmark where Lincoln delivered famous speeches and served as a state legislator.
5 Lincoln Tomb The final resting place of Abraham Lincoln, his wife Mary Todd Lincoln, and their sons.

Parks in Springfield

  • Washington Park: A large urban park with a zoo, botanical garden, and various recreational facilities.
  • Oak Ridge Park: A scenic park with hiking trails, picnic areas, and a golf course.
